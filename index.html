Reader picks

That our readers love

  • Fahrenheit 451

    This classic is what ignited my love of apocalyptic fiction. This novel that addresses censorship and book-burning in an authoritarian post-apocolyptic society is a must-read for everyone. "The magic is only in what books say, how they stitched the patches of the universe together into one garment for us."

    Ray Bradbury

  • Dublin Murder Squad Series

    I love thrillers, fictional crime procedurals , and mysteries of all types, and I've reread Tana French's Dublin Murder Squad series more than once. Each novel centers around a different case with a different protagonist who is a detective on the murder squad. Each novel is set in the same world, and side characters in one book become main characters in later novels.

    Tana French

  • The Invisible Man

    This is a shorter scif-fi story by H.G. Wells, but is beautifully written. The writing is concise and witty, as opposed to many sci-fi novels that tend toward drawn-out descriptions. It is a tale of the undoing of the prideful and arrogant physicist who manages to turn himself invisible, a character who is disagreeable, yet somehow still endearing.

    H.G. Wells

  • Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy

    Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy is a comedy sci-fi novel that is the only book that has ever made me laugh out loud. The writing is witty and irreverant, and is the kind of experience that makes you look at the world differently, and start seeing the humor in the absurdity of everyday life.

    Douglas Adams

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and Practicum's library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 visitors each month. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and making use of our ample online resources.

The library gets its name from Joan Feynman, an American astrophysicist and the first woman to be elected as an officer of the American Geophysical Union.
